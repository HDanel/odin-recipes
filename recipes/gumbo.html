Chicken Sausage Gumbo

Description

Whether it's on cold nights or any occasion, a warm bowl of gumbo always hits the spot. There are hundreds of different gumbo recipes throughout the history of Louisiana, but two main styles persist through today as synonymous with the dish. Chicken and Sausage Gumbo and Seafood Gumbo. This recipe is devoted for the former as a staple of gumbo served all across southern Louisiana, but especially in the plains area.

Ingredients

  • 1lb chicken breast and/or thighs
  • 1lb andouille sausage
  • 2TBSP canola oil (for meat)
  • 1 large white onion
  • 1.5 medium-large green bell pepper
  • 2-3 stalks celery
  • 1-2 dried bay leaves
  • 3/4 cup canola oil (for roux)
  • 3/4 cup flour (for roux)
  • dash Tony Chachere's Creole Seasoning
  • 2 cups dry white rice
  • 4 cups water (for gumbo body)
  • Alernatively, 4 cups chicken broth can be used
  • 2.5 cups water (for rice)
  • diced green onions for garnish
  • 3-5 cloves garlic (optional)
  • hot sauce for flavor, namely Crystals brand (optional)
  • 3-4 boiled eggs (optional)
  • dash gumbo filé (optional)

Steps

  1. Dice onion, celery, bell pepper, and garlic (if you like) and set aside in a big bowl.
  2. Boil chicken breast in about 4 cups of water until cooked (save the broth for later) or used pre-shredded chicken. Once cooked, shred the chicken and slice the sausage.
  3. Heat oil in a a pre-heated 2Q thick-bottom pot (a seasoned cast iron or magnalite pot are typically used).
  4. Brown meats together in the pot until browned on all sides. Set aside in a big bowl.
  5. Stir and mix together flour and oil in a small container until smooth
  6. Empty flour-oil roux mixture into the same pot used for browning the meats.
  7. Cook the roux mixture constantly on medium heat, stirring continuously as not to burn until preferred shade (many people in Acadiana prefer darker, thicker roux).
  8. Add previously diced vegetables and cook until onions are translucent, stirring occasionally
  9. Stir in previously browned meats and cover with chicken broth, mix well
  10. Mix in bay leaf, hot sauce, seasonings. Add boiled egg (optional).
  11. Cover and simmer for two (2) hours, taste-testing and adjusting seasonings or adding water as necessary
  12. Serve over freshly cooked rice and top with diced green onions
